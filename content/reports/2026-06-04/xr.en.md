## What happened

Ming-Chi Kuo says his prior Apple XR/smart-glasses roadmap is no longer a useful reference, with only two smart-glasses products still visible and Vision Pro successors 'off the table' [14][20][27]. Mark Gurman reports the 'Vision Air' was killed in Oct 2025, 'Display Glasses' in Jan 2025, AI glasses delayed to end of 2027, and AR glasses pushed out [10]; a Polymarket post frames the same split — display-less AI glasses on track for 2027, display AR glasses to 2029 [37]. Reaction items treat Vision Pro as effectively sunsetting and mock its small install base [28][35], though some still want a successor [32].

## Why it matters (reasoning)
Two independent supply-chain/analyst sources plus a market post pointing the same way [10][14][20][37] is a stronger signal than any single rumor, and it reframes near-term XR bets: the headset-first, high-end spatial-computing path is being deprioritized in favor of lighter glasses that won't carry a full visionOS-class app surface for years. Second-order effect for content makers: visionOS remains a live but shrinking-priority target — Apple is still funding the ecosystem (Design Awards [51], Steam streaming [16][39], Mac Virtual Display [4], immersive apps [46][47], a Berlin Developer Center [9]), so short-horizon client work is viable, but a multi-year Vision Pro-exclusive product is a weak commitment given an uncertain successor and tiny base [28][35]. Meta Quest, by contrast, is where shipping games and media volume sit [18][55], and tooling is broadening — Godot can now target XR and build on-device [3][50], lowering the barrier to non-Unity XR experiments.
