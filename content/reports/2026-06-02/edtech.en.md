## Why it matters (reasoning)
The clearest second-order effect is substitution pressure on fixed-curriculum apps. If general LLMs can generate personalized lessons from arbitrary source material [25][36], the moat of structured language/learning apps shifts from content toward habit, motivation, and trust — which is exactly why Duolingo is leaning on streak mechanics and amnesty [17][33] and expanding into Chess [21] to keep daily engagement. The streak/XP playbook traveling into fitness [29] and kids' STEM [18] confirms the durable, transferable asset in edtech is the engagement loop, not the subject matter. Meanwhile PW's pivot into lending [28] shows pure-content edtech economics are thin enough that players seek financial-services margin, and the bubble/coaching-harm critiques [13][14] are a reminder that engagement metrics and learning outcomes are not the same — a gap any serious edutech product has to defend against.
