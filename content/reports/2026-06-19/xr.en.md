## Why it matters (reasoning)
Two opposing forces are visible. On hardware, Snap's $2,195 standalone AR price shows that putting compute and battery into glasses-sized form factors remains expensive and unappealing to mainstream buyers, echoing earlier Vision Pro skepticism [1][18][20][36]. On platforms, the cost of building XR is dropping: Qualcomm's new Snapdragon Reality Elite chip is being designed into multiple headsets including Meta's 'Phoenix' [12][14], and the Android XR tooling/content stack (Godot 4.7, Android Studio, XREAL AURA, Samsung Galaxy XR) is consolidating around shared chips and SDKs [11][26][47]. The second-order effect for studios is that distribution and engines are converging on Android XR, so content can target several OEM devices at once. Display progress (Samsung RGB OLEDoS, 40,000-nit tandem panels) and rendering ports (UE5 Lumen GI to Vision Pro) point to better visual fidelity ahead [43][51]. Practical XR demand also keeps showing up in education, health and sports — ISS crew-health AR/AI, AR health experiences on Specs, and VR reaction-time training for athletes [4][15][27][35][44].
